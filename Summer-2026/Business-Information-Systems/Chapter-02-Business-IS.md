## Overview

In this chapter, we examine three foundational concepts of MIS:

* Business Processes
* Information Systems
* Information

We will explore how organizations use business processes to accomplish goals, how information systems support those processes, and how information differs from raw data. Understanding the relationship between these concepts is essential for understanding how modern organizations operate.

# Q2-1: What is a Business Process?

## Definition

A **business process** is a sequence of activities used to accomplish a specific organizational function or objective.

* Activities are individual tasks within the process.
* Activities work together to ensure work is completed efficiently and consistently.

### Example: Booking a Flight Online

A seemingly simple task involves many separate activities:

1. Enter travel dates and passenger information.
2. Search for available flights.
3. Select a preferred flight.
4. Enter passenger details.
5. Choose seats.
6. Add baggage requirements.
7. Complete payment.

Each activity contributes to the overall process.

### Why Business Processes Matter

Business processes help organizations:

* Complete tasks consistently
* Improve efficiency
* Reduce errors
* Ensure reliable outcomes

---

## Documenting Business Processes

Organizations document processes using standardized methods.

### Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)

**BPMN** is the global standard for documenting business processes.

Benefits include:

* Clear communication
* Consistent documentation
* Easier analysis and improvement

### Roles and Actors

Roles represent individuals or groups responsible for specific activities.

Each role performs a defined set of tasks within the process.

### Swimlanes

Swimlanes visually separate responsibilities between actors.

Benefits:

* Shows who performs each activity
* Clarifies handoffs between roles
* Improves process visibility

## How Much Detail is Enough?

When documenting a process:

* Include enough detail to avoid ambiguity.
* Exclude activities that are not essential for understanding the process.

The goal is clarity, not excessive detail.

---

## Why Organizations Standardize Processes

Organizations standardize processes to:

* Enforce policies
* Produce consistent results
* Improve scalability
* Reduce risk

Standardization helps ensure organizational rules and regulations are followed consistently.

---

## Key Takeaways

A business process is:

* A sequence of activities used to accomplish a function
* Commonly documented using BPMN
* Valuable for communicating and visualizing organizational workflows

# Q2-2: What is an Information System?

## Definition of a System

A **system** is a group of components that interact to achieve a purpose.

---

## Definition of an Information System

An **Information System (IS)** is a group of components that work together to produce information.

### Five Components of an Information System

1. Hardware
2. Software
3. Data
4. Procedures
5. People

Each component plays an important role in supporting organizational objectives.

## Why People Matter Most

Although technology receives significant attention, people are the most important component of an information system.

People:

* Design systems
* Operate systems
* Interpret information
* Make decisions based on information

---

## Using the Five-Component Model

The model can help you:

* Recognize your role in system success
* Ensure all components function properly
* Estimate the scope of new systems
* Identify implementation challenges
* Understand that information systems are more than technology

# Q2-4: How Do Structured and Dynamic Processes Vary?

Organizations use both structured and dynamic processes.

### Structured Processes

Characteristics include:

* Well-defined steps
* Predictable outcomes
* Consistent execution
* High standardization

Examples:

* Payroll processing
* Order fulfillment

---

### Dynamic Processes

Characteristics include:

* Less predictable workflows
* Frequent changes
* Greater flexibility
* Dependence on judgment and expertise

Examples:

* Consulting
* Product design
* Research projects

# Q2-5: What is Information?

## Common Definitions of Information

Information can be described as:

* Knowledge derived from data
* Data presented in a meaningful context
* Processed data
* A difference that makes a difference

---

## Information vs. Data

Information:

* Is more than raw data
* Varies from person to person
* Exists within individuals

Two people may interpret the same data differently and gain different information from it.

---

## Applying These Ideas

Understanding information helps you:

* Communicate more effectively
* Adapt to new systems
* Work successfully on teams
* Stay curious and continue learning
* Recognize that sharing data does not always mean sharing information

---

# Q2-6: What Are Necessary Data Characteristics?

High-quality data should be:

### Accurate

* Correct and free from significant errors.

### Timely

* Available when needed.

### Relevant

Relevant to:

* The context
* The subject being addressed

### Just Sufficient

* Provides enough information for decision-making.
* Avoids unnecessary excess.

### Worth Its Cost

* The value gained should exceed the cost of obtaining and maintaining the data.

## Practical Application

Success in business often depends on developing four key skills:

### Abstract Reasoning

Understanding and working with models and concepts.

### Systems Thinking

### Collaboration

Working effectively with others to achieve goals.

### Experimentation

Testing ideas, learning from results, and improving processes.

These skills require continual practice and self-motivation.

# Chapter Summary

Business Processes:

* Sequences of activities that accomplish organizational functions.
* Commonly documented using BPMN.

Information Systems:

* Systems that produce information.
* Consist of hardware, software, data, procedures, and people.

Information:

* Meaningful knowledge derived from data.
* Supports decision-making and organizational effectiveness.

Data Quality:

* Accurate
* Timely
* Relevant
* Just sufficient
* Worth its cost

Organizations achieve the greatest value when business processes, information systems, and information are aligned with organizational goals.
